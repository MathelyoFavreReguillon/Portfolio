FEA of Suspension Inserts

Finite element analysis of a double aluminium suspension insert integrated into a carbon monocoque, with the objective of validating stiffness, strength and safety under traction, shear and torsion load cases representative of Formula Student usage.

Project Overview

This project focused on the structural validation of a double suspension insert used as a fixation point between the wishbone assembly and the carbon monocoque of the EPFL Racing Team Formula Student car.

The work combined CAD-based geometry preparation, finite element modelling in 3DEXPERIENCE, mesh convergence studies, and comparison of the insert response under several critical mechanical load cases.

Key Contributions

  • Definition of a realistic FEA methodology for a suspension insert
  • Integration of traction, shear and torsion load cases
  • Development of mesh refinement and convergence workflow
  • Assessment of stiffness, von Mises stress and factor of safety
  • Identification of critical zones and design improvement directions

Context

The insert is part of the monocoque-side suspension interface of the Formula Student car. It connects the external suspension attachment to the main composite structure and must transfer high localized loads with limited deformation.

Because the insert works in a lightweight, high-performance environment, the design must remain compact and light while still ensuring reliable structural integrity in severe loading situations.

Geometry & Material

  • Double insert with two fixation holes for the suspension attachment
  • Overall dimensions: 94 mm × 54 mm × 21 mm
  • Two 13 mm holes for bolt passage
  • Mass around 29 g, volume around 10.35 cm³
  • Material: Aluminium 7075-T6
7075-T6 Material used for the insert
71.7 GPa Young’s modulus
503 MPa Yield strength

Methodology

The analysis was carried out in 3DEXPERIENCE using a 3D solid model of the insert. A linear static approach was used to evaluate the local stress state, displacement field and global safety of the component.

Symmetry conditions were exploited when possible to reduce computation time: a quarter model for axial traction, a half model for shear, and the complete part for torsion where no useful symmetry remained.

Load Cases

  • Traction case based on severe suspension load transfer
  • Shear case representing lateral loading on the insert
  • Torsion case to evaluate resistance to twisting effects
  • Conservative loading derived from suspension force studies
  • 40 N·m retained for the torsion study

Meshing Strategy

The model used quadratic tetrahedral elements, chosen to capture the complex geometry of the insert and the local stress gradients around holes, fillets and internal transitions.

The retained mesh combined a 3 mm global size with a 1 mm local refinement in critical zones. For the most demanding convergence studies, local refinements were pushed down to 0.25 mm and 0.1 mm depending on the load case.

Final Mesh Data

  • Global element size: 3 mm
  • Local refined size: 1 mm in critical areas
  • Total number of elements: 14,604
  • Total number of nodes: 27,226
  • Degrees of freedom: 81,678

Convergence Study

A dedicated convergence study was performed by progressively refining both global and local mesh sizes and comparing three key outputs: maximum displacement, maximum von Mises stress and minimum factor of safety.

This ensured that the selected mesh provided a stable and reliable basis for comparison between load cases, while keeping the numerical cost at a reasonable level for iterative design work.

Solver Setup

  • Linear static finite element analysis
  • General static step in 3DEXPERIENCE
  • Automatic incrementation enabled
  • Instantaneous elastic response assumption
  • Geometric nonlinearity activated when relevant

Main Results

Traction umax ≈ 0.283 mm, σmax ≈ 546 MPa, FoS ≈ 0.926
Shear σmax around 420 MPa, stabilized displacement, FoS ≈ 1.20
Torsion σmax ≈ 535 MPa, FoS ≈ 0.944, critical local overstress

Traction Case

In traction, the insert shows low global displacement and satisfactory rigidity. The most loaded areas are localized around the bolt passage and reinforced regions.

The maximum stress approaches the yield strength of 7075-T6 under the most severe loading assumptions, which makes this case close to the design limit.

Shear Case

In shear, the convergence study showed a stable displacement response and an acceptable minimum safety factor around 1.20 after mesh refinement.

A local stress concentration originally appeared near the junction between reinforcing bars and side walls, which led to the addition of fillets to reduce stress peaks.

Torsion Case

Torsion was the most critical case. The complete part had to be modelled, and strong local stress concentrations appeared in the internal transitions and around mechanical interfaces.

The minimum factor of safety dropped below 1, indicating that this loading scenario is a strong candidate for future local redesign or reinforcement work.

Engineering Takeaways

  • The insert is globally stiff and well optimized for most load cases
  • Critical local zones are strongly linked to holes, fillets and internal transitions
  • Mesh refinement is essential to obtain reliable local stress values
  • Torsion remains the most demanding case for the current design
  • The study provides a solid basis for future validation and redesign iterations
