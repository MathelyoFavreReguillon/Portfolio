Domino Laying Machine

Design and development of a manual mechanical machine able to place 50 dominos with a regular spacing, both in straight lines and curved trajectories, through a fully integrated system of storage, transmission, steering and deposition.

Project Overview

This project was carried out at EPFL in the context of a mechanical design course focused on the development of a complete functional system. The objective was to create a fully manual machine capable of placing dominos one by one with controlled spacing and stable vertical positioning.

The final design was developed around a strict specification including a capacity of 50 dominos, a target spacing of 20 ± 1 mm, compatibility with straight and curved paths, and compact overall dimensions below 300 mm in height.

Key Contributions

  • Concept generation and comparison of several storage, steering and deposition architectures
  • Mechanical design and CAD development of the final machine
  • Work on transmission, gearing, kinematics and integration of the full mechanism
  • Dimensioning and engineering calculations for steering, deposition and manufacturability
  • Participation in the overall development from concept phase to final assembly definition

Design Challenge

The main challenge was to synchronize multiple functions in a purely mechanical and manually driven system: storing the dominos, feeding them progressively, redirecting them, laying them vertically on the table and keeping a constant spacing regardless of the trajectory.

In parallel, the machine had to remain compact, stable, safe to use, easy to reload and compatible with both fixed and free steering modes.

Concept Selection

Several concepts were explored for the charger layout, transmission system, steering architecture and deposition principle. Among the investigated options were inclined storage, barrel-type solutions, horizontal chargers, belt transmission and different steering concepts.

The final concept selected a dual vertical charger architecture combined with a gear-based transmission, a dedicated projection mechanism and a centered deposition system judged to be the most reliable and feasible solution.

Final Mechanical Architecture

The machine stores 50 dominos through two vertical chargers of 25 dominos each. As the machine moves forward, a central driving wheel activates the internal transmission and powers the projection and deposition mechanisms.

A gear ratio of 2.5 was defined between the driving wheel and the actuation of the mechanisms, ensuring a regular deposition frequency while reducing the influence of curved trajectories on domino spacing.

Domino Deposition Workflow

Dominos are first pushed out of the two chargers by an alternating projection mechanism referred to in the report as the “windmill” system. They are then guided through dedicated slides which progressively rotate them from horizontal storage to vertical deposition.

A final crank-slider based deposition mechanism recenters the dominos coming from both sides and releases them through a single output, allowing vertical placement with controlled alignment and spacing.

Steering System

One of the strengths of the project is the integration of two steering modes. The machine can operate in a fixed steering mode with predefined trajectories, or in a free mode controlled by a steering wheel.

The fixed mode allows straight motion and predefined left and right turning radii of 200 mm and 400 mm, while the free mode gives more flexibility during manual guidance.

Engineering & Validation

The development involved more than CAD modeling. The report includes calculations for steering geometry, mechanism amplitude, gear loading, shaft torsion, friction losses, bearing efficiency, domino stability after release and maximum operating speed.

Materials and manufacturability were also considered carefully, with a large number of structural parts designed in PA6, low-friction interfaces in PTFE, aluminum shafts, and an assembly strategy compatible with machining.
