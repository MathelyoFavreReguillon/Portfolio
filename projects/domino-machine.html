EPFL · Mechanical Design Project

Domino Laying Machine

Design of a manual mechanical system capable of placing dominos with a controlled spacing, integrating storage, transmission, steering and deposition into a single compact machine.

Project Overview

The objective of this project was to design a fully manual machine capable of placing dominos one by one with a controlled spacing of 20 ± 1 mm.

The system was designed to handle up to 50 dominos, operate in straight and curved paths, and remain compact and stable.

Design Challenge

The main challenge was to synchronize storage, feeding, reorientation and deposition through a purely mechanical system driven by the movement of the machine.

The system had to ensure vertical placement, constant spacing and reliability across different trajectories.

Mechanical Architecture

The final concept uses two vertical chargers storing 25 dominos each. A central wheel drives a gear-based transmission system that activates the deposition mechanism.

A transmission ratio of 2.5 ensures a constant deposition frequency regardless of the trajectory.

Workflow

Dominos are pushed alternately from each charger, guided through slides that rotate them into a vertical position, and finally deposited through a centralized mechanism.

A crank-slider mechanism ensures alignment and controlled placement of each domino.
