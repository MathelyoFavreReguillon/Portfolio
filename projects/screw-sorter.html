ScrewSorter

Development of a compact automated screw sorting system for small and medium-sized workshops, where mixed fasteners are often discarded instead of reused. The project combined screw isolation, conveyor transport, camera-based measurement and automated bin distribution in a single integrated prototype, with a strong focus on sustainability, low energy use, practical manufacturability and batch-production positioning.

Key Contributions

  • Mechanical design of subsystems and final integrated prototype
  • Design progression from concept selection to proof of concept
  • Cost analysis and product positioning for batch production
  • Sustainability-oriented engineering for reuse and low energy consumption
  • Integration of sensing, control logic and manufacturable components

Performance

  • 12 screws/min sorting speed
  • 90% sorting accuracy
  • 57 W maximum instantaneous power
  • 600 × 900 × 280 mm final size
  • M3–M6 and L10–L70 screw range
  • 70% material sorting accuracy

System Architecture

The machine is structured around three main functions: screw isolation, camera-based measurement, and automated sorting.

A lifting system and one-by-one ramp feed screws individually onto a custom TPU conveyor, where the geometric features are measured before each screw is redirected toward the appropriate bin.

Closed-Loop Control

One of the key technical aspects of the project was the closed-loop control of the conveyor. Position feedback was used to stabilize screw transport and to ensure repeatable placement in front of the detection zone.

This control strategy improved synchronization between the transport mechanism, the measurement step and the sorting sequence, while reducing positioning uncertainty during operation.

Interface

A dedicated interface was developed to support system operation, testing and adjustment. It provided a practical way to interact with the prototype and to monitor the main stages of the sorting sequence.

This interface contributed to the overall usability of the machine by making calibration, debugging and demonstration phases clearer and more accessible during development.

Engineering Highlights

  • Custom TPU belt geometry for screw alignment and sensing
  • Closed-loop conveyor control using a distance sensor
  • Camera-based length and diameter estimation
  • Prototype optimized for compactness and workshop integration
  • Design trade-offs between throughput, robustness and complexity

Project Limitations

As a proof-of-concept prototype, the project also highlighted several technical limits. The camera-based detection remained sensitive to lighting conditions, positioning consistency and image interpretation robustness.

In addition, the dimensional precision of the 3D-printed TPU belt influenced repeatability and transport quality. These aspects, together with calibration stability and classification reliability, defined the main improvement areas for a future iteration.

Cost & Sustainability

The single prototype cost was estimated at 262.55 CHF, while the projected production cost for a batch of 100 units was 187.59 CHF per unit, leading to a target selling price of about 230 CHF.

The project was framed around reuse, reduction and rethink principles, with the objective of reducing screw waste, manual sorting effort and associated environmental impact.
