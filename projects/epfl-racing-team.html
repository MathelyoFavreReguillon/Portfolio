EPFL · EPFL Racing Team · Second Year

Monocoque & Layup Division Lead

In parallel with my Vice-President role, I led the Monocoque & Layup Division, overseeing one of the most critical manufacturing scopes of the car. This position combined technical ownership, production planning, process structuring and representation of the division internally and externally.

Production leadership

Monocoque Production Organization

I structured and supervised the production of the new carbon monocoque from preparation to execution, with dedicated tools for pre-production, layup follow-up and post-production.

This included organizing the manufacturing logic, sequencing the work, documenting the process and making sure the division could deliver reliably on one of the most critical structural components of the car.

Composite innovation

Lightweight Composite Boxes

I also supervised the production of several electronic housings and implemented my own composite box concept to achieve lighter, stronger and faster-to-produce parts.

The method relied on laminating a single flat carbon plate, CNC cutting the parts and assembling them by bonding. This enabled a fast production flow while delivering robust housings that successfully passed all competition waterproofness tests.

Technical representation

Design Justification & Technical Reviews

A key part of the role was being able to justify technical choices, manufacturing decisions and design logic to highly qualified judges and technical reviewers.

This required strong understanding of the parts, the processes behind them and the ability to explain and defend the work with clarity in a demanding engineering context.

External exposure

Sponsoring & JEC Representation

I also contributed to the visibility of the division by presenting our work externally, including sponsor-oriented exchanges and representation of the chassis / monocoque scope at JEC.

This strengthened my ability to present technical work in a professional way, connect engineering content with external partners and support the team’s sponsorship dynamics.

Leadership

Management of a Large Student Engineering Team

As Vice-President, I worked within a team structure gathering around one hundred members across many technical and organizational scopes.

The role required listening to many different perspectives, taking people’s feedback into account and helping decisions converge in the most efficient way for the project as a whole.

Sponsors

Sponsor Events & External Representation

I participated in sponsor-facing events and exchanges that helped strengthen the team’s visibility and relationships with partners.

This included events and meetings linked to JEC, Emil Frey and Garrett, as well as broader interactions with industrial stakeholders around the team and the project.

Operations

Team Logistics & Standardized Inventory

I was responsible for a significant part of the team logistics, including the implementation of a standardized inventory system at team scale.

This work aimed at improving visibility, organization and readiness by structuring storage, references, equipment tracking and competition-related material management.

Testing

Test Days Logistics

I contributed to the logistical preparation and execution of test days, including planning, transport organization and material readiness.

These phases required anticipation, coordination and execution discipline to ensure that tests could run efficiently and support the broader vehicle development process.

Event management

Rollout Organization

I helped organize the team rollout, the official reveal of the car to families, partners and guests.

This involved coordinating the venue, catering, transport, equipment, decoration, sound and overall event logistics for a large-format presentation gathering roughly 300 people.

Competitions

Competition Logistics

I also contributed to the organization of competition logistics, covering transport plans, administrative preparation, ATA carnet follow-up and detailed planning.

This was essential to ensure that the team, the car and all key material could move efficiently and reliably across events and international competition environments.
