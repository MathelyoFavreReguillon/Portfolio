Monocoque & Layup Division Lead

During my second year in the EPFL Racing Team, I took on the role of Monocoque & Layup Division Lead. This position placed me at the center of one of the most critical manufacturing scopes of the car, combining production organization, technical responsibility, team coordination and direct involvement from manufacturing to competition support.

Monocoque Production Organization

As division leader, I structured and supervised the production of the new carbon monocoque from preparation to execution. This role required building a clear manufacturing logic, anticipating constraints, organizing the work between members and ensuring that each step was progressing with the required level of rigor.

I worked on the full production approach, including pre-production organization, layup follow-up and post-production management. The objective was not only to manufacture the part, but also to make the whole process more reliable, more readable for the team and better controlled from start to finish.

Lightweight Composite Boxes

In parallel with the monocoque scope, I supervised the production of several electronic housings and implemented my own concept of composite boxes in order to obtain lighter, stronger and faster-to-produce parts.

The manufacturing method relied on laminating a single flat carbon plate, CNC cutting the different elements and assembling them by bonding. This approach made the production flow significantly faster while keeping a high level of robustness, and all the boxes successfully passed the waterproofness requirements during the competitions.

Design Justification & Technical Reviews

A major part of the role was being able to justify technical choices, manufacturing decisions and design logic to highly qualified judges and technical reviewers. This required not only understanding the parts in detail, but also mastering the reasoning behind the process choices, constraints and trade-offs.

I had to present the work in a clear and professional way, explain why certain solutions had been selected and defend the consistency of the manufacturing approach in a demanding engineering environment.

Sponsoring & JEC Representation

I also contributed to the external visibility of the division by presenting our work to partners and by representing the chassis / monocoque scope during sponsor-oriented exchanges and at JEC.

This part of the role strengthened my ability to communicate technical work beyond the workshop, connect engineering topics with industrial partners and support the broader sponsorship dynamics of the team.

Vehicle Assembly with Other Divisions

As an active member of the team, my role did not stop at the production of monocoque-related parts. I also took part extensively in the assembly of the car together with the other divisions, which gave me a much broader understanding of how the vehicle came together as a complete system.

Working closely with other areas of the team was essential to make integration progress efficiently, solve practical issues on the car and keep the monocoque scope aligned with the realities of the final assembly phase.

Competition Monitoring of the Monocoque & Cockpit

During the three summer competitions, I followed the condition of the monocoque and cockpit closely in order to ensure that these areas remained reliable and competition-ready throughout the events.

This follow-up was carried out during the competitions in Switzerland, Germany and France, and required attention to detail, responsiveness and the ability to react quickly if any issue appeared on the car in an intense operational environment.

Management of a Large Student Engineering Team

During my second year in the EPFL Racing Team, I took on the role of Vice-President. This position gave me a broad transversal view of the project and required me to operate across technical, organizational and logistical topics in order to support the team’s overall execution.

Working within a structure gathering around one hundred members meant listening to many different perspectives, understanding the needs of multiple divisions and helping decisions converge in the most efficient way for the project as a whole.

Sponsor Events & External Representation

I took part in sponsor-facing events and external exchanges that helped strengthen the visibility of the team and maintain strong relationships with industrial partners. This role required representing the project in a professional manner while adapting the message to the audience and the context.

This included participation in events and meetings linked to JEC, Emil Frey and Garrett, as well as broader interactions with stakeholders around the team. These experiences helped me connect technical work, project identity and partner expectations more effectively.

Team Logistics & Standardized Inventory

I was responsible for a significant part of the team logistics, including the implementation of a standardized inventory system at team scale. The objective was to improve visibility on available material, make storage more coherent and support smoother preparation for tests, events and competitions.

This work involved structuring references, organizing equipment tracking and bringing more consistency to the way material was stored and managed across the team. Beyond logistics alone, it contributed to making the organization more reliable and more efficient in day-to-day operations.

Test Days Logistics

I also contributed to the preparation and execution of test days by organizing planning, transport and equipment readiness. These sessions were important milestones for the development of the car and required careful anticipation to make sure the team could use testing time effectively.

This meant coordinating practical details, checking that the required material was available and ensuring that the logistical side supported the technical objectives of the day. It was a role strongly linked to execution discipline and operational reliability.

Rollout Organization

I helped organize the team rollout, the official reveal of the car to families, partners and guests. This event represented an important moment for the team because it showcased the year’s work while also reflecting the image, seriousness and ambition of the project.

My involvement covered the logistical and organizational side of the event, including venue coordination, catering, transport, equipment, decoration, sound and overall preparation for a large-format gathering of roughly 300 people. It required both anticipation and attention to detail to ensure a professional final result.

Competition Logistics

I also contributed to the organization of competition logistics, covering transport plans, administrative preparation, ATA carnet follow-up and detailed event planning. This was a critical aspect of the season because competition performance depends not only on the car itself, but also on how well the full team operation is prepared.

Ensuring that the team, the car and all key material could move efficiently and reliably across events required structured planning, anticipation of constraints and strong coordination. This part of the role gave me valuable experience in managing complex logistics under real operational pressure.
