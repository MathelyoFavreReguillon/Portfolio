EPFL · EPFL Racing Team · Second Year

Vice-President

In my second year within the EPFL Racing Team, I combined two major responsibilities: Vice-President of the team and leader of the Monocoque & Layup Division. This role placed me at the intersection of technical execution, manufacturing leadership, team coordination and project organization.

Role Overview

As Vice-President of the EPFL Racing Team, I contributed to the overall management of a large student engineering organization combining technical development, manufacturing, logistics, planning and competition preparation.

This position required balancing strategic vision with operational execution, while helping the team stay aligned across departments and throughout the race car development cycle.

Main Responsibilities

  • Supporting overall team coordination and decision-making
  • Contributing to planning, organization and project follow-up
  • Helping align technical progress with manufacturing reality and deadlines
  • Supporting team structure, communication and day-to-day operations
  • Taking part in competition preparation, tests and global execution

Leadership Dimension

This role pushed me to grow beyond pure engineering execution. It required leadership, prioritization, communication and the ability to help people move in the same direction in a demanding and fast-paced environment.

It was especially formative in understanding how performance also depends on team structure, project discipline and the quality of coordination between many parallel workstreams.

Impact

Being Vice-President gave me a much broader perspective on what it takes to bring a race car project from design to production, testing and competition.

It reinforced my ability to connect technical challenges with organizational realities, which is essential in any high-performance engineering environment.

Role Overview

As leader of the Monocoque & Layup Division, I was responsible for one of the most critical parts of the car: the manufacturing and delivery of the carbon monocoque and the organization of the layup-related work.

This role combined composites manufacturing, technical supervision, production planning and coordination of people working on one of the most demanding components of the whole vehicle.

Main Responsibilities

  • Leading the Monocoque & Layup Division throughout production
  • Supervising composite manufacturing activities and preparation steps
  • Coordinating people, tasks and priorities within the division
  • Ensuring manufacturing quality, process consistency and progress tracking
  • Contributing to the final delivery of a key structural part of the race car

Technical Dimension

The monocoque is at the center of the car architecture, both structurally and from a manufacturing perspective. Leading this division required understanding the full production chain, from preparation and layup to organization, execution and manufacturing reliability.

It was a role where technical rigor, anticipation and workshop discipline were essential, because delays or errors on such a part have major consequences on the rest of the project.

Team & Production Leadership

Beyond the technical side, this role also meant leading people in a production environment: assigning work, maintaining momentum, solving day-to-day issues and keeping the division aligned with the larger team schedule.

It was one of the most intense and formative leadership experiences of my time in the team, because it required both engineering understanding and strong execution under pressure.
